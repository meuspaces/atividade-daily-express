Affecting Millenials

Although millenials may seem to have a greater investment in health and welness than
generations before them, findings from the Blue Cross Blue Shield Association (BCBSA)
suggest millenials will be substantially less healthy as they age.


The report defines the following as the the top 10 conditions affecting millenials, ranking by
adverse health impact:


  1. Major Depression
  2. Substance use disorder
  3. Alcohol use disorder
  4. Hypertension
  5. Hyperactivity
  6. Psychotic conditions
  7. Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis
  8. Hight cholesterol
  9. Tobacco use disorder
  10. Type 2 diabetes

"While the top 10 conditions affecting millenials are not necessarily surprising, what is
shocking in the prevalence rates for each of these conditions in millenials when
compared to rates for previous generations, "Dr.Vincent Nelson , vice president of medical
affairs for the BCBSA, told Heathline.


Millenials aged 34 to 36 in 2017 were 11 percent less heathly than Gen Xers aged 34 to
36 in 2014, and had a double digit increase in diagnoses for eight of Top 10 health
conditions.


Still, 83 percent of the 55 millions millenials surveyed in 2017 considered themselves
in good or excellent health, despite the BCBSA analysis revealing otherwise.


"Because significant health challenges are rising among millenials earlier than in
previous generations, we mus address these issues now, "said Nelson.

Mental health conditions hit millenials hardest

When compared to the national population, millenials are more affected by hehavorial
health conditions than physical, with the highest increases shown in rates of major
depression and hyperactivity.


"While we may be seeing moderate diagnosis impact across all generations for previously
stigmatized behavioral health conditions, millenials are seeing higher growth in
prevalence than either Gen X or baby boomers, "said Nelson.


Major depression, substance abuse disorder, and alcohol use disorder were the top three
contitions for millenials.


Deborah Serani, PsyD, professor at Adelphi University and award-winning author of
"Living with Depression," says the prevalence of these conditions among millenials
doesn't surprise her.


She believes the following circumstances in which millenials grew up are contributing
factors:

  1. Technological advances

    Because of technology, millenials were the first generation to grow up without learning
    how to maintain eye contact, become adept at reading facial expressions, or deepen
    awareness of the textures of emotions within themselves or others.

    "This lack of emotionals awareness, clinically called alexithymia, makes is hard for
    millenials to understand their thoughts and feeling,"Serani told Heathline.

How healthcare can change to help more millenials

To gain a better understanding of the decline in millenials's health accross all illnesses,
Nelson says BCBSA hels sessions with companies accross the country.

He says the following were takeaways about how to better address current millenial
lifestyles:

  • Millenials want to be met where they are with access to their own health records and convenient care,
    including telemedecine-they don't want another app to download.

  • Because millenials are disproportionately affected by behavorial health conditions, the prefer a holistic
    approach to their health that integrates both mind and body.

  • Millenials have had a double digit increase in diagnoses for eight of the Top 10 ealthconditions.

  • Millenials would rather work in environments able to address behavioral health and its underliying
    issues without stigma.


When it comes to depression, Nelson says while 89 percent of American adults consider
depression a very serious condition, the majority believe not enough
resources are being allocated to depression.

"One particularity troubling trend we've identified is that 1 and 5 millenials diagnosed with
major depression are not seeking any form of treatment, "said Nelson. "BCBSA's study of
major depression found the condition can rob people of 9 or more years of heathly life, so
it's imperative to allocate research and resources to new treatments as more millenials
are diagnosed with this and other behavorial helath conditions."
